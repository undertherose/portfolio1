The Project
An Infographic about Hamburger

An infographic about hamburgers. Some fun facts, some nutrition data, some history knowledge, how burgers changing in the US and vary around the world. And, how to make a burger.

Role & Team

Team Size: 5

My Role: UI/UX Designer, mainly focused on the agenda view and daily view.

Others' Roles: Designers

Tools Used

Design Tools: Figma, Adobe Illustrator

Researches: Competetor Analysis

Challenge
The Models

I chosed the infographic “New Year’s By The Numbers” as my visual language model and typography model. It contains variant types of lines and illustrations and two fonts.

I also used this infographic as my layout model. It experiments with horizontal and vertical sections. The whole infogrphic is connected with illustrations. My color model, “Hospital”, has a light grey background, dark grey texts and lines, a lot of white color and two pop colors, yellow and orange.

Appearance
Start

The visual potential of appearance is exploited to convey the basic components of a typical burger. I started with using pictures of real burgers, capturing its figure, and emulating the models.

Improvements

Relationship
Start

I started by showing how hamburgers are related to sandwiches and hotdogs. But by doing that I used a lot of patterns that don’t exist in my visual language model, like overlays, outlines with fills, etc.

Improvements

To emulate my visual language model, I decided to do the process of making a patty instead, and the patty can easily join the big burger. But I had a really hard time to emulate my two visual language and color model at the same time. I tried a lot of different designs and finally find one that looks nice.

Time
Start

The time display shows a sequence of events, the new trend of hamburgers every ten years from 1920 to today, in the United States. I first tested two different displays, one vertical and one horizontal. But the lines and arrowhead in the display looked different than my visual language model.

Improvements

To emulate my visual language model, I got rid of the arrowhead, changed the thickness of the line. But the spacing between timespot is off and I tried to space them equally but the display took up two much space. In the end, I decided to edit the words, move the display to a new place and add in some illustraions.

Number
Start

At the beginning, the display showed the number of burgers consumption per capita across countries. I tried a bar chart and a pie chart. The pie chart really stood out to me becuase it looks like a tomato slice and it could become another part for the big burger. But the data I used made more sense being a bar chart. So I decided to swich the displayed data and make a tomato slice.

Improvements

My visual language model also makes all its elements into real things. At first the color and structure of the slice weren’t work and causes confusion. So I redesigned the illustration and added more details so it looked like tamato slice. Then I twisted it to add some angle so it looked like part of the burger.

Location
Start

The information I want to showcase is the variations of burgers across the world. I started by designing a world map with tags of information on it. But the design looked really crowded and hard to read.

Improvements

I tried to get rid of the outlines, but then the map was really hard to see on the background. I added a slice of cheese as background but this didn’t help. After I added the text information, the design became overcrowded again.
